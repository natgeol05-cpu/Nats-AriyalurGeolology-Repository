Sphenoceramus

Description

Name: Sphenoceramus
Formation: Karai Formation (Karai 'badlands')
Locality: Near Aiyanavaram.
Classification: Molluscs: Bivalvia: Order- Pteriida: Family - Inoceramidae: Genus - Sphenoceramus

Sphenoceramus is a genus of extinct, gigantic inoceramid clams that lived during the Late Cretaceous period. These fossils are characterized by a unique shell sculpture with concentric folds that can be intersected by oblique radial ribs.
Sphenoceramus species, like the massive S. steenstrupi, have been found globally and are considered important index fossils
for dating specific geological strata. Some species may have survived in low-oxygen environments, potentially with the help of
symbiotic bacteria, and some fossils have been discovered near cold seeps.

Key characteristics and lifestyle • Genus: Sphenoceramus is a genus within the extinct family Inoceramidae.
• Time period: Lived during the Late Cretaceous period, with some fossils from the Santonian to Campanian stages.
• Size: Known for being gigantic, with some species like S. steenstrupi reaching enormous sizes, up to 2 meters in height.
• Shell structure: Characterized by concentric folds that are often intersected by oblique radial ribs.
• Habitat: Found worldwide in marine sediments, suggesting wide dispersal and possibly long-lived larvae.
• Some species were abundant in low-diversity or low-oxygen environments.
• Symbiosis: Evidence suggests some species may have had chemosymbiotic bacteria in their gills,
• which could have helped them survive in oxygen-depleted waters or near cold seeps.

• Index fossil: Due to its recognizable shell sculpture and distinct time range,
Sphenoceramus has been used as an index fossil to help correlate rock layers globally.
• Fossil associations: The presence of Sphenoceramus in certain strata can indicate
specific paleoenvironmental conditions, such as the presence of cold seeps.
• Geographic range: Fossils have been found in locations such as Japan, Russia, Canada, Greenland,
and the United States, providing valuable data for understanding global geological history.

The scientific classification for Sphenoceramus is: Kingdom: Animalia, Phylum: Mollusca, Class: Bivalvia,
Order: Pteriida, Family: Inoceramidae, and Genus: Sphenoceramus.
This classification places it within the V larger group of extinct inoceramid bivalves, which were related to modern clams and mussels.
